## Outbreak Details

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) has reported a listeria outbreak affecting multiple states, with the following key points:

- 28 people have fallen ill across 12 states ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667))
- All 28 affected individuals have been hospitalized ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667))
- 2 deaths have been reported, one in Illinois and one in New Jersey ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667), [AP News](https://apnews.com/article/listeria-deli-meat-cdc-deaths-916ee047b0df234d860afc406928ee86))
- Cases have been reported between May 29 and July 5, 2024 ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667))
- New York has the highest number of cases (7), followed by Maryland (6) ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667))

The CDC warns that the actual number of cases may be higher, as some people recover without medical care and are not tested for listeria ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667)).

## Source of the Outbreak

While the exact source is still under investigation, the outbreak has been strongly linked to deli meats:

- 16 out of 18 interviewed patients reported eating meats sliced at deli counters ([USA Today](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/health/2024/07/19/listeria-outbreak-deli-meat-cdc/74477170007/), [ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667))
- Most commonly mentioned meats were deli-sliced turkey, liverwurst, and ham ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667))
- No specific products or brands have been identified yet ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2024/07/20/nx-s1-5047253/listeria-outbreak-deli-meat-cdc))
- There is currently no evidence of illness from prepackaged deli meats ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667))

## At-Risk Groups

Listeria infection can be particularly dangerous for certain groups:

- Pregnant women and their unborn babies ([Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/listeria-infection/symptoms-causes/syc-20355269))
- Adults aged 65 or older ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667), [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/listeria-infection/symptoms-causes/syc-20355269))
- People with weakened immune systems ([Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/listeria-infection/symptoms-causes/syc-20355269))
- Individuals with conditions such as diabetes, kidney disease, or those undergoing chemotherapy ([Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/listeria-infection/symptoms-causes/syc-20355269))

## Symptoms and Health Risks

Listeria infection can cause various symptoms:

- Fever, muscle aches, nausea, and diarrhea (common symptoms) ([Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/listeria-infection/symptoms-causes/syc-20355269))
- Headache, stiff neck, confusion, loss of balance, and convulsions (if infection spreads to nervous system) ([Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/listeria-infection/symptoms-causes/syc-20355269))
- Symptoms can appear anywhere from the same day to 70 days after consuming contaminated food ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667))

For pregnant women, listeria can lead to miscarriage, stillbirth, premature birth, or life-threatening infection in newborns ([Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/listeria-infection/symptoms-causes/syc-20355269)).

## Preventive Measures

The CDC and health officials recommend the following precautions:

- High-risk individuals should avoid eating meat sliced at deli counters unless heated to an internal temperature of 165°F (73.9°C) or until steaming hot ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667))
- Practice good food safety habits, including thorough hand washing and proper food handling ([Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/listeria-infection/symptoms-causes/syc-20355269))
- Cook foods thoroughly and use a food thermometer to ensure safe temperatures ([Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/listeria-infection/symptoms-causes/syc-20355269))
- If you experience symptoms of listeriosis and have recently consumed deli meat, contact your healthcare provider ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Health/cdc-warns-listeria-outbreak-linked-deli-meat-left/story?id=112110667))
As the investigation continues, it's crucial to stay informed about updates from health authorities and take necessary precautions to prevent listeria infection, especially if you belong to a high-risk group.
