## üì¢ Recall Details

On July 12, 2024, Wiers Farm Inc. of Willard, Ohio, issued a voluntary recall for a limited number of whole cucumbers and bagged salad cucumbers due to potential contamination with Listeria monocytogenes. This decision was made out of an abundance of caution to ensure consumer safety. The recall affects whole cucumbers with a pack date of June 5, 2024, and bagged salad cucumbers with pack dates of June 5 and June 6, 2024. For more detailed information, visit the [FDA website](https://www.fda.gov/safety/recalls-market-withdrawals-safety-alerts/wiers-farm-inc-issues-voluntary-recall-whole-and-salad-cucumbers-due-possible-contamination-listeria).

Consumers who have questions or concerns about the recall can contact Wiers Farm Inc. at (419) 933-2161 or via email at customercare@wiersfarm.com. For more information about Listeria monocytogenes, consumers can visit the [CDC website](https://www.cdc.gov).

## ü¶† About Listeria

Listeria monocytogenes is a bacterium that causes listeriosis, a serious foodborne infection with a high case-fatality rate of 20-30%. This infection primarily affects individuals with impaired cell-mediated immunity, including those who are pregnant, elderly, or immunocompromised. Listeriosis can manifest in two primary forms: febrile gastroenteritis and invasive listeriosis. Symptoms include muscle aches, fever, and gastrointestinal distress. In severe cases, the infection can spread to the nervous system, causing more severe symptoms.

## üõ°Ô∏è Safety Measures

To protect yourself from Listeria and other foodborne illnesses, it is crucial to follow proper food safety practices. One of the most effective measures is to ensure that your refrigerator is kept at or below 40¬∞F (4¬∞C) and your freezer at 0¬∞F (-18¬∞C). Listeria can survive and even grow at refrigerator temperatures, so maintaining the correct temperature is essential.

Regularly clean your refrigerator, countertops, and other kitchen surfaces to prevent cross-contamination. Use a solution of one tablespoon of liquid chlorine bleach per gallon of water to sanitize surfaces that have come into contact with potentially contaminated foods. This practice helps eliminate Listeria and other harmful bacteria that can linger on surfaces.

When handling food, always wash your hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds before and after preparing food. This simple step can significantly reduce the risk of transferring harmful bacteria from your hands to the food you are preparing.

Cook foods to their proper internal temperatures to kill harmful bacteria. For example, cook poultry to an internal temperature of 165¬∞F (74¬∞C), ground meats to 160¬∞F (71¬∞C), and leftovers to 165¬∞F (74¬∞C). Use a food thermometer to ensure that these temperatures are reached.

Avoid consuming raw or unpasteurized dairy products, as these can be sources of Listeria. Always choose pasteurized milk, cheese, and other dairy products to reduce the risk of infection. Additionally, be cautious with ready-to-eat foods like deli meats and hot dogs, which should be heated to steaming hot before consumption.

Be mindful of food recalls and safety alerts. For instance, Wiers Farm Inc. recently issued a voluntary recall of whole and salad cucumbers due to possible Listeria contamination. Staying informed about such recalls can help you avoid consuming contaminated products.

If you suspect that you have consumed a contaminated product, monitor your health closely for symptoms of Listeria infection, such as muscle aches, fever, and gastrointestinal issues. Seek medical attention promptly if you experience these symptoms, especially if you are pregnant, elderly, or have a weakened immune system.
